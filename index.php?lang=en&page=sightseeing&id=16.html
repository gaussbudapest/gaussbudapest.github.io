16. State Opera House

The opera-house is one of the most beautiful public-buildings in Hungary. You can find the Opera House on Andrássy Boulevard. It was built in neorenaissance style, between 1875-1884 on the basis of Miklós Ybl’s plans.

Its front is decorated with columns, arcades and statues. On the two sides of the driveways you can see the statues of Ferenc Erkel and Ferenc Liszt. These two famous Hungarian Musicians sat for the artist for these statues. Still on the front there are 16 other statues of famous opera-writers such as Mozart or Verdi.

The horseshoe-shaped, three level high auditorium is one of the most magnificent ones in Europe and it also has perfect accoustics. Above the auditorium there is a beautifully decorated chandelier. The huge fresco on the ceiling was painted by Károly Lotz. This is the largest wall-painting of Hungary: with an outer perimeter of 45m. The circle-shaped composition shows the Olympus with important gods and goddesses. The technical equipment (turning-stage, trap-doors) of the building was extremely modern at the time it was built. And it was still unique 100 years later.

The Hungarian State Opera House is also unique in Europe because it is awaits lovers of opera and ballet not only in the autumn-winter season, but also during BudaFest, in the beginning of August.
