20. Széchenyi Chain Bridge

As the first permanent link between Buda and Pest, the majestic Széchenyi Chain Bridge has a special place in the heart of locals, for whom it is a symbol of civic endurance. Austrian troops tried and failed to destroy it in 1849, but in 1945 the bridge fell victim to the Wehrmacht, who dynamited all of Budapest’s bridges in a bid to check the Red Army. Their reconstruction was one of the first tasks of the postwar era; the Chain Bridge was reopened on November 21, 1949, on the centenary of its inauguration.

The bridge was the brainchild of Count István Széchenyi, who had a passion for innovation, who founded the Academy of Sciences and brought steam engines to Hungary, amongst other achievements. Designed by William Tierney Clark, it was constructed under the supervision of a Scottish engineer, Adam Clark (no relation). The two river piers were built in Classicist style, they carry the road of the bridge, hanging on the iron chains, hence the name Chain Bridge. Clark personally thwarted the Austrian attempts to destroy it by flooding the chain-lockers. After his rescue party, he became a real Hungarian patriot. The engineer settled in Buda, married a Hungarian woman and lived there until his death in 1866.

GE Lighting donated the new lightning system of the bridge to Budapest for the 150th anniversary of the Chain Bridge. The new lighting system preserved the evening look of the bridge, at the same time it uses the most modern lighting solutions in order to minimalize the costs of electricity usage and maintenance. In order to preserve the morning look, the lamps on the gates were dyed into the colour of limestone, like the colour of the stones, and the lamps on the ironwork got the same grey colour as that of the bridge.

The city government indicated the need of a new, more complete restoration in 2002. According to a revision, which was carried out in 2011, the insulation, the covering and the ferro-concrete deck slab should be replaced. After that, BKK announced a tender for the entire renovation of the Chain Bridge, the Adam Clark Square and the Tunnel of the Castle. According to the plans of 2014, the work could be started as early as the autumn of 2016 and would have been finished by 2019.

The Chain Bridge became one of the most important symbols of Budapest that is why its image is an essential part of the presentational materials of Budapest or Hungary. The image of the Chain Bride appears on the 200 HUF coins among others. A historical film with the title of The Bridgeman, which is one of the highest budget Hungarian films, also deals with the topic of the birth of the bridge.
