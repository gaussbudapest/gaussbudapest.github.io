29. Margaret Island

Margaret Island, which is basically made up of several small sandbanks and has an area of 1.5 km2, has been the spot of many remarkable events throughout its 2000-year-old history. A Roman watchtower once stood at its northern point. In the Middle Ages it was not only an outstanding hunting ground, but also gave sanctuary to women: the Dominican nuns treated as moral or social outcasts were looking for the quality of life in devotional work and care of the sick. The nuns' houses were converted into a monastery by King Béla IV after he had brought her daughter, Margaret, from Veszprém to this place, from whom the island actually takes its name. The island became the residential property of the palatines during the 18th century, who transformed it into a beautiful park replete with rare plants. Great works of literature have also drawn inspiration from its beauty: we may find references in Őszikék by János Arany and in the poem 'Toldi'. The legendary island, where duels used to be held, is now a peaceful, green scene within the busy and noisy capital city. So it's no wonder the Grand Hotel was built in the 19th century at the northern side joining the Árpád Bridge, which welcomes the guests wishing for tranquility and recovery together with the neighbouring Thermal Szálló. There are swimming pools as well as open-air performances. Nevertheless, the island offers excellent leisure facilities (e.g. jogging, playing chess and 'ulti'). Therefore, Margaret Island is the most popular park in Budapest.
